Checkpoint Mall is Rogue Point’s largest and most sprawling map, and the second oldest and most iterated. I was responsible for the map across its many developments, from building the initial blockouts to several rebuilds as the shape of the game started to take form.

In many ways, the Mall is the quintessential Rogue Point map. Though it is a smidge Frankensteinian in nature, this adds to its unique charms. Due to its vast size, it feels like several maps, and this is actually how we use it in conjunction with our [Modular Randomization System]({% link _projects/rogue-point/randomization.md %}). Each shop and main area serves as its own small map, so on Easy difficulty missions we constrain you to these areas individually. The level then really opens up on higher difficulties, where we can navigate you through several of these in directed and interesting ways.

{% include gallery.html items=page.mall_gallery_1 gallery_name="mall1" %}

The level suffered quite the direction crisis as we built it. It had originally been built as a linear journey from the Car Park into the Mall, with subsequent difficulties forcing you deeper. Across testing however, this experience felt repetitive, because for example, a Medium mission would effectively be the Easy mission you may have already just played, plus an extra 30% at the end.

This is where the [Modular Randomization System]({% link _projects/rogue-point/randomization.md %}) and its strengths began to take form, as we reconceptualized the Mall instead as a map which can be approached from a huge variety of angles and shapes. This let us get truly creative with how layouts were built, but one downside was that it necessitated the propagation of lots of different “back hallways” and “back rooms” so that players could safely spawn in and navigate into the main spaces. These were not present and accounted for in initial builds because they were not necessary for flow in the those versions. This caused some consternation between both design and art and proved to be quite the speedbump in development. Nonetheless, we this challenge navigated well and eventually the map became the most varied map in Rogue Point, and a bit of a fan favourite.

One aspect that I feel we particularly nailed is that all the different arenas in the map feel very distinct from each other, especially on a visual level. We present you with a lot of options in any given arena; both in terms of ways to circumvent combat, but also either to confront the Merx head on, or stealthily approach a situation. Unfortunately, the stealth aspect of our game never quite took off, but if it had, this map would have been ready for it.
