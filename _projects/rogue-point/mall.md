Checkpoint Mall is Rogue Point’s largest and most sprawling map, and the second oldest and most iterated. I was responsible for it across its developments, from building the initial blockouts across several rebuilds.

In many ways, the Mall is quintessential Rogue Point map. Though it is a smidge Frankensteinian, this adds to its unique charms. Due to its vast and sprawling nature, it feels like several maps, and this is actually how we use it in conjunction with our Modular Randomization System. Each shop and main area can really serve as its own small maps, so on Easy difficulty missions we constrain you to these areas. The level then really opens up on higher difficulties.

We built each shop and major area as their own unique combat arena

The level suffered quite the direction crisis as we built it. It had originally been built as a linear journey from the Car Park into the Mall, with subsequent difficulties forcing you deeper. Across testing however, this experience felt rather repetitive, because for example, a Medium mission would basically be the Easy mission you may have already just played, plus an extra 30% at the end.

This is where the Modular Randomization System and its strengths began to take form, as we reconceptualized the Mall instead as a map which can be approached from a huge variety of angles and shapes. This let us get truly creative with how layouts were built, but one downside was that it necessitated the propagation of lots of different “back hallways” and “back rooms” that player could safely spawn in and navigate into the main spaces. These were not present and accounted for in initial builds because they were not necessary for flow in the those versions. This caused some consternation between both design and art and proved to be quite the speedbump in development, but one which we navigated well and eventually the map became the most varied map in Rogue Point, and a bit of a fan favourite.

One aspect that I feel we particularly nailed is that all the different arenas in the map feel very distinct from each other, especially on a visual level. We present you with a lot of options in any given arena; both in terms of ways to circumvent combat, but also confront the Merx head on, or stealthily approach a situation. Unfortunately, the stealth aspects of our game never quite took off, but if it had, this map would have been ready for it.
