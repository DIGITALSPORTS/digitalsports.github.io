Surface Tension: Uncut is a great point of personal pride for me. Back when Black Mesa was released as a free mod, I was a passionate Half-Life fan who had been following along since its inception. I was disappointed by the cuts the team had made to their interpretation of the Surface Tension chapter and decided to put my Level Design skills to the test by remaking those levels myself and releasing them publicly. It was my own first publicly released Level Design project and was very well received by the community. It was also the reason why Crowbar Collective eventually decided to hire me. Imagine my joy when it was decided we would integrate my mod into the base game for its upcoming commercial release!

We decided on a ground-up remake to better meet the standards of our retail release, which I happily dove into head-first. I personally feel that our take on these levels brings them from the weakest parts of the original chapter to the best parts of our version. For our version, I really focussed on bringing to the fore the sense of “all-out war” between the Xenians and the HECU (soldiers), both through dramatically over-the-top scripted sequences and through subtle environmental storytelling.

This set of maps was a tour-de-force by all our departments and really taught me how to coordinate and work with each individual discipline. I was responsible for most aspects of these maps throughout their development, from the initial blockouts, to the scripting and gameplay refinements, to several art passes: integrating art from other departments and implementing cinematic physics sequences.
