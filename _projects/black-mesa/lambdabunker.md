Lambdabunker in Half-Life: Deathmatch has the dubious distinction of being a 1:1 rip from the first level of the singleplayer chapter “Forget About Freeman”. As such, we were not sure if we would include it in our multiplayer or not, and the version we were initially going to include was also a 1:1 rip of our own version.

I wanted to flesh out our multiplayer map roster, so I rebuilt this level from the ground-up and gave it the proper multiplayer treatment, respecting the shape of the original but adapting it into a flowing multiplayer layout. I also decided to artistically differentiate it from the original level, which was set during a time when the Black Mesa facility is being destroyed by artillery strikes. The multiplayer level is instead set in this event's aftermath, which I think was a great artistic choice that gave the level an eerily calm feel, contrasting nicely with the chaos of multiplayer.

This map taught me a lot about multiplayer combat flow. The original singleplayer map had many dead-ends which required rectification to ensure a smooth and uninterrupted combat experience. Sometimes this was simple, through the addition of basic connecting hallways, other times this required building large and complex areas to preserve the balanced feel of the level. I really love the way this map flows in the final build, you can comfortably loop around the level gathering resources and fighting opponents in a really natural and fluid way. A tough choice that I was particularly proud of with this map was cutting the entire sewer section of the original level, as I felt it detracted from the tightly designed looping structure of the surface level.
