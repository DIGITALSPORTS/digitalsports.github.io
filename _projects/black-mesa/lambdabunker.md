Lambdabunker in Half-Life: Deathmatch has the dubious distinction of being a 1:1 rip from the first level of the singleplayer chapter “Forget About Freeman”. As such, we were not sure if we would include it in our multiplayer or not, and the version we were initially going to include was also a 1:1 rip of our own version.

At some point in development, I wanted to flesh out our multiplayer map roster, so I decided to rebuild this level from the ground up and give it the proper multiplayer treatment, respecting the layout of the original but making it suitable for a flowing multiplayer layout. In the process I also decided to artistically differentiate it from the original level. The original singleplayer level is set during a time when the facility is being destroyed by artillery strikes. I decided to set the multiplayer version of the level in the aftermath of this, which I think was a great artistic choice that gives the level an eerily calm feel that contrasts nicely with the chaos of multiplayer.

This map taught me a lot about multiplayer combat flow. The original singleplayer map had many dead-ends which needed to be rectified to ensure a smooth and uninterrupted combat experience. Sometimes this was simple, through the addition of basic hallways, other times this required building large areas to preserve the balanced feel of the level. I really love the way this map flows in the final build, you can just infinitely run around the level gathering resources and fighting people in a natural and fluid way.

A tough choice that I was particularly proud of with this map was cutting the entire sewer section of the original level, as I felt it detracted from the nice looping structure of the surface level I had built.
